## THE SERIES ENGINE (why these are one shelf, not unrelated romances)

Every book runs the **same fundamental beat**, refracted through a new couple:

> Someone the **machine** turned into an asset — a **product** (sold as an image) or a **machinist** (paid to build images) — meets a **refuser** (someone who treats people as unpriced persons), and through them **steps outside the machine into found family.**

The machine = the **image economy** (music label makes the face / fashion house brands it / media conglomerate airs & monetizes it; it values the image over the person). Full spine: **BIBLE §CANON LOCK — THE MACHINE.** Placement of any character = the **four slots** (product / operator / machinist / refuser); the charge in each book = a character **moving between slots** (the machinist’s “turn,” the product who refuses to be sold, the operator who’s discarded and becomes a person).

**The spine that runs through all books = the machine + the boards (Subplot Y).** This is where Book 1’s *banked* board/inheritance war goes active: the greedy collateral relatives, shareholder blocs, and governance fights are a **renewable antagonist** powering later books, so Book 1 stays tight (boards = pressure only) and the saga-scale conflict has somewhere to live.
