> **What this is.** The K-pop-industry research, compressed into one reference. It is *iceberg* — the world you know while writing Do-yun, not material that appears on the page. You draft **from** it; you never quote it.

## 1. The machine, in one breath

Three corporations — music label (makes the face), fashion house (SEORÉ, dresses it), media conglomerate (Daon) — are three gears of one image economy that **turns people into priced images and values the image instead of the person.** It discards what it can’t price. The training pipeline is how a child becomes priceable: years of evaluation, ranking, monitored bodies, banned wanting. Do-yun is the machine’s perfect output. Jae is what it discarded twice (faceless catering girl; no-name accountant). The book’s win condition isn’t smashing the machine — it’s doing the one thing it can’t process: **being a person, in the open, for free.**

Every tell below is residue of that pipeline. None of it is his diagnosis. All of it is his *habit.*

## 2. The tells (diagnosis → gesture)

What years inside the machine actually left in his body — rendered as things Jae sees and files under **“spy,” “expensive habits,” “off.”** Suspicion, never tenderness, until the wall fails.

- **Scored-for-precision reflexes.** Rinses rice until the water runs clear without being taught; squares the edges of a stacked thing; redoes a small task that was already fine. *(Trainee execution drilled past muscle memory.)* Jae files: *who does chores like there’s a judge.*
- **Involuntary self-review.** Hums a run under his breath and corrects it mid-hum; catches himself and stops. *(Watch-your-own-tapes conditioning.)* Jae files: *spy can’t stop rehearsing something.*
- **Stillness as the tell, not motion.** Fingers drum when he’s dysregulated; go flat-still when he’s regulated *(with her).* The calm is the anomaly, not the fidget. Track which state each scene is in.
- **Exit-mapping.** A phone raised across a room, a stranger’s too-long look, a camera — and something in him recalculates the doors. *(Sasaeng/leak residue.)* Reads on the page as a man who always knows where the exits are. Jae files: *spy habits.*
- **Usefulness as the safe channel.** Defaults to service — coffee, ramyeon, fixing the thing, shrinking to fit her walls. *(The machine paid for output, never received a gift.)* This is the load-bearing one: it’s why being *given* to (her coffee, three years ago) detonated him, and why uncoded care wrecks him now.
- **Trained charm that doesn’t cash.** The smooth surface runs fully in command (Ch03 towel beat) — but the dating ban meant it never became experience, so **actual contact undoes him.** He’s never more obviously untouched than right after he’s been most smoothly a rogue. (Heat = mouth-vs-body split; already locked in BIBLE.)
- **The asset-flinch.** A beat of going blank/compliant when handled — the posture of someone whose hand-broken-or-not came *after* the cleanup got signed. Surfaces when his mother, the label, or Hyun “manages” him.

## 3. The two wounds — keep them surgically separate

The single most important non-negotiable in the research, and it’s already canon:

- **The song wound = private, her-shaped, frozen 3 years.** “Under” starts the coffee-night week and freezes where she vanishes. She is its missing end. Moves when she’s near, freezes when she’s gone.
- **The voice wound = public, product-shaped, broken by the party.** A senior director gropes a catering girl; he swings because it’s wrong, splits his knuckles, is erased-by-cleanup before anyone asks if his hand is broken. This breaks his *public voice* (“vocal rest”).

They are **not the same wound.** The song bridges the Ch22 exposure, not a name or a face. Do **not** let the research’s general “trauma” wash them into one ache.

**Firewall (Option A) — do not breach:** he knows store = diner = roommate. He does **not** know the faceless party girl was Jae (banked to the Ch30 dark moment). No tray / ballroom / catering / manifest language in his chapters connecting Jae to the party. His party-fear is *generalized* (“my world erases women who get near me”), never an identification.

## 4. The sharpest hook in all of it: rigging = audit

The Produce-101 vote-rigging math (every total a multiple of one hidden base number; uniform repeating gaps where independent votes should scatter) is the machine’s whole thesis in miniature: **a hidden hand scaling people into a predetermined outcome and calling it a fair count.**

Jae is an *auditor.* “Every total is a multiple of the same ghost number” is a discrepancy she’d catch cold. That is a real bridge between her trade and his wound — her plain language for *fraud* is the air his entire industry breathes. When this depth first needs to surface, it surfaces **here**: not as him explaining the scandal, but as her clocking a rigged ledger in terms that accidentally name what was done to him. He goes still. She doesn’t know why. The reader does.

This is the beat the research keeps circling. It’s the one to spend.

## 5. The song / creative process — the one genuine open gap

The research’s “gaps” doc was right about exactly one thing: the artistic process is thin. But it’s **internal canon, not external research** — no essay about anyone’s self-producing model tells you why *“Under”* freezes. What’s locked vs. open:

- **Locked:** “Under” is unfinished *by design* (dies on the cliff); open-window take (Ch18) is the leak; resolution is a separate late indoor scene (~Ch32, present ≈ February); runtime 2:38, kept out of prose; texture warm/jazz-inflected (Rachael Yamagata / “Something in the Rain”); moves-near / freezes-gone.
- **Open (decide, don’t Google):** the *texture* of him writing — sterile studio vs. raw flat; what the machine does to the act of composing; what specifically the song can’t reach without her (a line? an outro lift? a single true word he won’t write under the ban?). This is the only “research” left worth doing, and it’s a writing decision.
