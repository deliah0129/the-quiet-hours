# THE QUIET HOURS — House Style (Banter-Forward), Candidate Spec

**One line:** First-person, dual-POV, banter-forward contemporary romance — fast, funny, self-deprecating, dialogue-carries-everything, feeling-named-then-undercut. The BookTok-commercial lane (Tessa Bailey / Christina Lauren / Ali Hazelwood), retooled for an INFJ + an idol so the *extrovert engine sits in Do-yun* and the *dry restraint sits in Jae.* This is a texture layer that would replace the Rice × Steel layer, not sit on top of it (see §7).

-----

## §0 — TENSE: PAST *(locked)*

This register is most itself in **past tense.** The direct-address asides, the retrospective winks, the “rules” annotations, the *for-the-record* editorializing — they all breathe more naturally when the narrator is telling you a story that already happened.

But *The Quiet Hours* is currently locked **present**, across ~15 drafted chapters and the POV/motif engine.

**Decided: past tense,** to match the uploaded source. This unlocks the full retrospective-narrator comedy — the *for-the-record* asides, the rules that annotate the plot, the wink over the shoulder — and it costs a tense + person conversion pass on the ~15 drafted chapters, taken chapter by chapter.

*Tense audition — same Jae aside, both ways:*

> **Present:** He hums a four-count thing under his breath, like a man scoring a film only he can see, and I add it to the folder.
> **Past:** He hummed a four-count thing under his breath, like a man scoring a film only he could see, and I added it to the folder. For the record, the folder was extensive.

The past-tense version can lean on *for the record, the folder was extensive* in a way the present can’t quite. That’s the whole tradeoff in one line.

**Also note:** both the source voice and your existing locked samples are **first person** — so first-person is consistent. The drafted chapters are third, so adopting this means a person-conversion either way.

## §1 — The voice in one paragraph

A narrator who is *performing for the reader* while lying to the room. The scene moves on dialogue; description is sparse and functional; thought drops in as a fast aside and gets out — usually on a joke. Feeling is real but rationed: it surfaces, then gets escape-hatched with humor before it can sit. The banter *is* the flirtation; antagonism and attraction live in the same line. Warm, loose, lightly profane, genre-aware, and built to be heard.

-----

## §2 — Standing rules (the constitution)

1. **Dialogue carries the scene.** Long volleys, often **tag-free**, tracked by rhythm and voice alone. When a thought and a line of dialogue compete, give it to the dialogue.
1. **Low interiority, high momentum.** Stay in the room, not the head. Interiority arrives as a quick aside, not a paragraph, and exits fast.
1. **Name the feeling, then undercut it.** Sincerity is allowed for one beat, then deflected with a joke. The reader feels it *because* she won’t sit in it. (“It was everything I wanted in a man, honestly. It just sucked that he was Sebastian.”)
1. **The narrator talks to the reader.** Direct address, confiding asides, *for-the-record* editorializing. You and the narrator are co-conspirators against the other characters.
1. **Comedy is the medium, not the seasoning.** No scene is wall-to-wall ache. Heaviness lands *because* it’s surrounded by light.
1. **The world talks — the ensemble is a comic chorus.** Secondary cast exists to heckle, escalate, and out the heroine’s feelings she won’t admit. Every secondary voice instantly recognizable; plot smuggled in under the back-and-forth.
1. **One 🎧 standalone audio exchange per chapter** — a ~30-second dialogue beat that crackles read aloud, on its own.
1. **MBTI runs underneath, never on the surface.** Felt, never named.
1. **Logic is load-bearing.** Every beat survives a skeptical reread. Track canon in the story-state log.

## §3 — The full feature set

**Skeleton (structure & stance)**

- First person, dual POV, alternating leads.
- Tropes worn openly: accidental roommates → contract marriage, forced proximity, the meddling family, the secret identity, the grumpy/sunshine split.
- **Chapter titles as punchline “rules”** — alliterative, comment on the plot, joke + thesis at once (“Rule 18: babies should not mix with baseball players, not if you’re trying to keep the fake fake”).
- Self-aware genre wink — the book knows it’s a romance and nudges you.

**Muscle (the comic engine)**

- Sentence **fragments as timing** — the punchline gets its own line. (”…One sugar.”)
- **Hyperbole and pop-culture asides** — present/timeless jokes popping out of the narration.
- **Comic cataloguing / call-and-response riffs** — stacked setup-payoff (“Cha-cha slide? Yes. Macarena, nailed it. The floss? No.”).
- **Repetition & callback** — verbatim echo for escalation; lines return as punchlines.
- **The verbal spar IS the flirtation** — no separate tender register; the bickering carries the longing.
- **Physical awareness rendered comically, not lyrically** — the body gets a beat, then a deflection. Never lush, never lingered on.

**Connective tissue (texture & calibration)**

- **Dropped dialogue tags** — rapid-fire volleys with no “he said,” the *absence* a deliberate choice.
- **Confessional-to-the-reader** — frank with *us* while denying it to the room, in the same breath.
- **Casual, lightly profane diction** — the voice swears; register stays loose and contemporary. (Calibrate to taste, but don’t write it prim.)
- **Heat is tension-forward, not explicit** — proximity and longing over on-page spice; fade-friendly; the verge holds.
- **Cozy / found-family texture** — small community, warmth, comfort under the comedy.

## §4 — POV engine & the his/hers split *(the critical adaptation)*

Both leads narrate in first person, in the same house voice — but **two temperaments.** And the polarity is **flipped from the source:** in the transcript the *heroine* is the motormouth and the *hero* is the dry one. Here it’s the reverse, because you can’t make an INFJ the extrovert.

**Do-yun = the extrovert engine (the “heroine-flavor” of the source voice).**
Fast, verbal, charming, fills silence, performs-then-reads-the-room, processes *out loud and outward.* His chapters move quickest and carry the most banter and the most jokes — and it’s plausible, because charm is his trained surface. The longing is enacted in how relentlessly he attends to her.

> The trick to a stranger’s kitchen is to be useful before they’re awake enough to throw you out. I have made coffee. I have made *good* coffee — the kind that says *low-maintenance, easy to live with, definitely not hiding from his entire family.* I moved my cup to her side of the shelf, which is either a romantic gesture or a hostage situation, and I won’t know which until she sees it.
> She sees it.
> “I had a cup,” she says.
> Not a no. I’ll take a not-a-no. I have built whole years on less.

**Jae = the dry introvert (the “hero-flavor” of the source voice).**
Terse out loud; her power is the confessional aside, not the volley. She *clocks*, she doesn’t quip. The comedy is the deadpan gap between what she sees and what she’ll say. Her interiority stays — but in this register it runs **fast and dry and short-burst, not lush** (this is the one place she diverges hardest from the old spec).

> Here is what I know about my new roommate after one week. He pays cash, two months up front. He dyes his own hair in my bathroom and rinses the evidence down my drain. He owns a watch worth more than my education. And he hums — a four-count thing, under his breath, like a man scoring a film only he can see.
> I have a theory. The theory is *spy.* The theory is airtight and I have a folder.
> The folder is a problem for another day.

**The two-hander — the spar carries it, tags dropped:**

> “You reorganised my shelf.”
> “I *curated* your shelf.”
> “There are two cups touching.”
> “They’re cold. They huddle for warmth. It’s very sad. You should look away.”
> She doesn’t look away. Neither do I. The kettle gives up before either of us does.

**Arc movement:** his river can *slow* and go sincere as the real man surfaces; her dryness can *crack* and let real warmth through as the wall lowers. Movement in the ratio = movement in the relationship.

**Canon guard (carry-over):** early-arc, Jae’s confessional-to-reader stays in **suspicion / irritation**, never admiration — she’s frank with us about how *annoying* and *suspect* he is, not how she wants him. The rueful “he’s everything I’d want, it just sucks that it’s him” confession is a **late-arc** unlock, earned only once the wall is failing. Don’t spend it early.

## §5 — Heat calibration

Tension-forward. The charged detail — a half-inch of cup neither of them moves, a belt buckle, the not-closing — does the work. Explicitness is rationed and earned; the verge holds. Fade-friendly.

-----

## §6 — Per-scene checklist

- Is the scene carried by **dialogue**, not narration about it?
- Did interiority arrive as a **fast aside** and leave on a beat, not settle into a paragraph?
- Did at least one feeling get **named then undercut**?
- Is there a **direct-to-reader** confiding moment?
- Did a **fragment** land a punchline on its own line?
- Did the **ensemble** heckle / escalate / out a feeling somewhere?
- Is the body **noted-and-joked-past**, not made lush?
- Does **Do-yun run faster/funnier** and **Jae dryer/sparer**?
- Is Jae’s reader-confession **suspicion, not admiration** (until the arc earns the flip)?
- One standalone **🎧** exchange present, and does it carry read aloud?
- Does the **chapter title** double as a joke and a thesis?

## §7 — What this supersedes / keeps vs Rice × Steel

**Ports directly (already shared between both docs):**

- Comedy & light required; the world talks / ensemble runs dialogue-forward; one 🎧 standalone exchange per chapter; audio-first; logic load-bearing; MBTI underneath; tension-forward heat / verge engine; the his-vs-her *asymmetry* doing wound-work.

**This replaces (the texture flip):**

- “Scene is the medium, interiority is seasoning; lush, saturated, Rice-cadence; senses lead, lush-but-anchored” → becomes **low interiority, high momentum, comic asides, sparse functional description.** The prose stops being *lush* and becomes *fast.*
- Do-yun’s “Rice-forward, devotional, sensory-saturated river” → becomes **the verbal/charming/funny extrovert engine.** (Same function — he’s the one who pours outward — different surface.)
- Jae’s “Rice-rich perception, torrential interiority” → becomes **dry, fast, short-burst confessional interiority.** She still notices everything; she just reports it like a wry witness, not a poet.

**The single irreconcilable choice:** *lush* and *fast* can’t both be the house default. This spec picks *fast.* If you want to keep any lushness, the cleanest place to bank it is the rare, late, sincere beats — the moments the comedy deliberately steps aside — rather than the baseline texture.
