## 1 · LOGLINE

A burnt-out K-pop idol and luxury-fashion heir, hiding from his own life under a plain name, secretly sublets a stranger’s spare room — and the stranger on the other side of the wall is the woman whose one act of kindness three years ago he never recovered from, and whom his own world has erased. Forced proximity → secret contract marriage → the buried truth that she is the stolen heir his family helped disappear. Slow-burn, restraint-forward, audio-first.

## 2 · SPINE & STRUCTURE

- **Act 1 — accidental roommates.** The hideaway; the collision; the wall between them.
- **Act 2 — secret contract marriage.** Proximity turns social, legal, domestic at once.
- **Dark moment** — exposure forces the marriage public **AND** she learns the contract was secretly *rigged* (decided-for-in-the-dark: her deepest wound).
- **Act 3** — he tears up the rigged terms and hands her the free choice; she chooses him **in the light.** **HEA.**
- The **heir-reveal** is a late-act catalyst that **tests** the love, never rewards it.

> ⚠ **Open at the spine level:** the *secret marriage to Jae* and the *public merger to Seo Yuna* are not yet reconciled (they can’t legally coexist). Proposed model + the sequencing fork are in **§13**.

- **The firewall:** the public does **not** know idol “Seungwoo” = SEORÉ heir Kang Do-yun. Linking them = **triple scandal** (secretly heir + secretly arranged-married + secretly forced to retire). All publicity is sequenced to protect this.

The **label** wants the reverse — keep him performing, deliver the comeback. KAIROS’s self-produced record (their autonomy bid) dies if he’s retired.

## 10 · CRAFT SYSTEM

- **World-as-it-is:** report the world as objective fact; strip the perception-filter; decentralize the POV character as grammatical subject; interiority is a **marked dip,** never the lens.
- **Hybrid POV:** third-person-limited, **present tense**, alternating leads by chapter (her = odd, his = even); first-person *italic* surfaces at charged beats. Her chapters interior-leaning; his cinematic with the camera on her.
- **Monologue discipline:** interiority capped at 1–2 sharp lines per dip; if a feeling needs more, externalize it into dialogue/action/the world. Dialogue + world stay dominant.
- **De-forcing:** characters never explain their own/each other’s roles, backstory, or theme aloud; bonds show through behavior + unexplained shorthand.
- **Heat:** restraint-forward; awareness, irritation, held dialogue — not decorative description. **Two distinct currents:** eyes = soul/recognition; body = animal/attraction. ~3/5 by Ch7, earned.
- **Audio-first:** rhythm in the punctuation; sound/smell/temperature over visual; scene breaks as breath; **one ~30-sec standalone exchange per chapter.**
- **Motifs:** her scent-read of his **vetiver & cedar**; his **beats** vs her **numbers**; the **given-name** jolt (he says “Jae-wu,” never “Ms. Yun,” never the nickname); the **rain.**
- **Chapter ceiling: 6,000 words** (her chapters tend to run ~3.5–4.5k).

# CANON LOCK — THE MACHINE (the image economy) + THE FOUR SLOTS (2026-06-09)

*The thematic/worldbuilding spine. The three corporations are not separate industries — they are three gears of ONE machine, and every character is placed by their position relative to it. Sits above Subplot Y (the boards).*

## The machine = the image economy

All three powers sell the same product — **desirable images of people, delivered to consumers** — they just own different stages of one assembly line:

- **Music label** (Noh’s co., owns KAIROS) = **makes the face** — manufactures the idol/image (the raw content).
- **Fashion house** (SEORÉ) = **brands the face** — ambassador/maison prestige turns a performer into aspiration-you-can-wear. *(On the page already: Do-yun = Cartier-style ambassador.)*
- **Media conglomerate** (Daon) = **broadcasts & monetizes the face** — owns the pipe (TV, music shows, streaming, ad inventory); where attention becomes money.

**The loop:** label makes image → fashion brands it → media airs/sells it → revenue + fame fund the next image. Each gear is useless without the other two → **partners by economics, rivals by margin** (this grounds the “shaky alliance / enemy of my enemy” in industry logic, not just personalities).

**The nature of it (the antagonist is a PROCESS, not a villain):** the machine **turns people into priced images and values the image instead of the person.** Image = the currency; the machine is what mints it out of human beings. It **discards what it can’t price** (= why Jae was erased twice — faceless catering girl, no-name accountant). To beat it you can’t smash it; you do the one thing it can’t process — **be a person, in the open, for free** (no image, no price, no market value = the Te-proof event that unmakes the mother).

**Do-yun = the AXLE.** All three gears need him at once: SEORÉ heir (fashion, by blood) + label idol (music, by contract) + viral image (media, by exposure). Not *in* the machine — the thing it turns on. **Jae = the other hub** (controls the pipe once revealed as Daon heir). **The marriage fuses the face + the channel** → owns the two ends the operators can’t function without → the checkmate, and the step *outside* the machine.

## The four slots (places anyone, named or new)

1. **PRODUCT** — turned into images and sold. **Do-yun** (purest, the axle), **Jae** (channel-kind, post-reveal), **Yuna** (merger-bride = a face traded for a deal). Wound: *only my image; the person is unseen.*
