## The his/hers split — the thing most often gotten wrong

Both leads narrate first person, same house voice, **two temperaments. The polarity is flipped from a typical rom-com:**

- **Do-yun = the extrovert engine.** Fast, verbal, charming, fills silence, performs-then-reads-the-room, processes outward. His chapters carry the most banter and jokes (charm is his trained idol surface). His longing shows in how relentlessly he attends to her.
- **Jae = the dry introvert.** Terse out loud; her power is the confessional aside, not the volley. She **clocks, she doesn’t quip.** The comedy is the deadpan gap between what she sees and what she’ll say. Her interiority stays, but runs **fast, dry, short-burst — never lush.**

**Never give Jae a motormouth voice.** She is an INFJ; the verbal engine belongs to Do-yun. In a two-hander, he carries the talk and she answers short; her scarce words land harder for the contrast.

## Canon guard on Jae — protect this

Early- and mid-arc, Jae’s confessions-to-the-reader stay in **suspicion / irritation, never admiration.** She is frank with us about how *annoying* and *suspect* he is — not how she wants him. She has not admitted attraction to herself; she still frames him as an adversary. **Never let her narration call him beautiful or leak tender admiration.** When attraction surfaces physically, she explains it away (chemistry, a cold night, an animal body) or frames it as a threat to her self-sufficiency. The rueful “he’s everything I’d want, it just sucks that it’s him” confession is a **late-arc unlock**, earned only as the wall fails. Don’t spend it early.

## Voice constitution

1. **Dialogue carries the scene** — long volleys, often tag-free, tracked by rhythm. Thought loses to dialogue when they compete.
1. **Low interiority, high momentum** — stay in the room. Interiority arrives as a fast aside and exits, usually on a joke.
1. **Name the feeling, then undercut it** — one sincere beat, then a deflection. The reader feels it *because* she won’t sit in it.
1. **The narrator talks to the reader** — direct address, confiding asides, *for-the-record* editorializing; narrator and reader are co-conspirators against the room.
1. **Comedy is the medium, not the seasoning** — no scene wall-to-wall ache; heaviness lands because it’s surrounded by light.
1. **The world talks** — ensemble (KAIROS, neighbors, the diner, the agency) is a comic chorus that heckles, escalates, and outs feelings she won’t admit; plot smuggled under the back-and-forth; every secondary voice instantly recognizable.
1. **MBTI underneath, never named.**
1. **Logic is load-bearing** — every beat survives a skeptical reread; track canon in the state log.

## Toolkit (use, don’t checklist mechanically)

Fragments as comic timing (punchline on its own line) · hyperbole & pop-culture asides · comic cataloguing / call-and-response riffs · repetition & callback as escalation · the verbal spar *is* the flirtation · physical awareness noted-and-joked-past, never lush · dropped dialogue tags · casual, lightly profane diction (don’t write it prim) · cozy / found-family texture.

- **Heat: tension-forward, not explicit.** The charged detail (a half-inch of cup neither moves) does the work; the verge holds; fade-friendly.
- **Chapter titles are “rules”** — a joke on the surface, the chapter’s thesis underneath (e.g. “Rule Eleven: Do not, under any circumstances, let the spy make you coffee”).
- **One 🎧 standalone audio exchange per chapter** — a ~30-second dialogue beat that crackles read aloud on its own.
