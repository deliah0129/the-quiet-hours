## 7. THE CADENCE TRAP (standing drafting guard)

The uploaded research carries an **AI-essay cadence that is NOT the book’s voice** and will fight it if it rides in on borrowed texture. Tells: adjective-stacked atmosphere, triadic sensory lists, the breathless “…and held it,” and above all **do-the-thing-then-gloss-it** (a punchy closing line that names what the scene meant — “No technique. Just truth.” / “It felt like remembering.” / “Some doors stayed locked from the inside.”). That gloss is exactly what show-don’t-tell cuts — even inside Do-yun’s lush channel. **Pull the shape of the move; leave the cadence and the gloss.** End scenes on the shown thing (the snapped pencil), not the moral.

## 8. HOW THE AUTHOR WORKS (apply going forward)

- **Reads research as a mood board, not facts.** She’s sourcing register, texture, tone, and *lean* (which subject area she’s circling) — not canon. Name the lean and lift the usable *move*; the canon/voice conflict rides along as a footnote, never the lead. She often already knows a doc breaks canon and doesn’t care.

- **Reserves line-level creative authority.** A word that hits sideways, a deliberate odd choice, a double entendre. Claude drafts a **floor, not a ceiling**; never silently “corrects” a chosen word back to the expected one (ask once if genuinely unsure); protects *both* meanings of a double entendre (the buried one dies first); and remembers audio-first — a second meaning must be carried by sentence/structure/punctuation, not by spelling the eye can see. **The line is hers, full stop.**
