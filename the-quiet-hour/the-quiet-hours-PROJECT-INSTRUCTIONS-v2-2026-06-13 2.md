## Locked register — literary slow-burn, banter dial UP

- **First person, past tense, dual alternating POV.** (Jae = odd chapters; Do-yun = even.)
- **Register = literary slow-burn with the banter dial UP (banter-hybrid).** The slow-burn interiority, restraint, the Jae Guard, the charged-beat discipline, and show-don’t-tell are the **substance**. Banter is the **surface**, and it is turned up: the dialogue sparks, Do-yun’s extrovert charm runs, the ensemble heckles. The two are not in tension — comedy frames the ache; the ache is why the comedy matters.
- **The sentence-level engine is the register split** (`register-split.md`): one house style, split by POV — **Do-yun pours, Jae withholds** — and the split *is* the why-them, rendered in prose. Do-yun’s lush channel (atmospheric, sensory, the held breath) blooms on charged beats and pulls back to plain between them. Jae’s dry channel (concrete nouns, short bursts, the feeling turned over for the bill before it lands) withholds by default; her rare lush line is a late-arc detonation earned by twenty chapters of restraint.
- **The banter has a floor it can’t cross.** It (a) never displaces the dry interiority or the restraint — banter is the surface, the slow-burn is the substance; (b) never lands *on* the charged beat — it **surrounds** the beat and goes **silent** for it (the poured water, the heartbeat under her palm, the song moving), lets it play clean, then resumes (“It’s a quieter nothing.”); (c) never climbs into Jae’s narration — she is the deadpan *gap*, never the volley.
- **Dual anti-drift — both directions are errors.** ✗ Do **not** revert to “banter-forward commercial / dialogue-carries-everything” (banter as *substance* — the conversion misread that nearly wrecked the prose). ✗ Do **not** austerify the banter back out to match a “pure / austere slow-burn” label (the spark is canon as of 2026-06-13; quieting it is reverse-drift). The dial sits between, deliberately.
- **Retired registers.** The lush 3rd-person-present “Rice × Steel” house style (`house-style.md`) is retired; existing 3rd-present chapters are *pending conversion*, not the target. `house-style-comedic.md` is a **banter-toolkit reference only** (mine it for comic mechanics) — **not** the master register doc. Master = `register-split.md` + this dial.

## The his/hers split — the thing most often gotten wrong

Both leads narrate first person, one house voice, **two temperaments. The polarity is flipped from a typical rom-com:**

- **Do-yun = the extrovert engine.** Fast, verbal, charming, fills silence, performs-then-reads-the-room, processes outward. His chapters carry the most banter and jokes (charm is his trained idol surface). His longing shows in how relentlessly he attends to her. His interiority, when it comes, **pours** — lush, sensory, on the charged beat.
- **Jae = the dry introvert.** Terse out loud; her power is the confessional aside, not the volley. She **clocks, she doesn’t quip.** The comedy is the deadpan gap between what she sees and what she’ll say. Her interiority stays, but runs **fast, dry, short-burst — withheld by default, never lush** (until the late-arc unlock).

**Never give Jae a motormouth voice.** She is an INFJ; the verbal engine belongs to Do-yun and to the room. In a two-hander, he carries the talk and she answers short; her scarce words land harder for the contrast. Banter sparking in the dialogue is Do-yun’s and the ensemble’s job — it must not migrate into Jae’s narration.

## Canon guard on Jae — protect this

Early- and mid-arc, Jae’s confessions-to-the-reader stay in **suspicion / irritation, never admiration.** She is frank with us about how *annoying* and *suspect* he is — not how she wants him. She has not admitted attraction to herself; she still frames him as an adversary. **Never let her narration call him beautiful or leak tender admiration.** When attraction surfaces physically, she explains it away (chemistry, a cold night, an animal body) or frames it as a threat to her self-sufficiency. Kinship-tenderness toward shared wounds is allowed; romantic/aesthetic admiration is not. The rueful “he’s everything I’d want, it just sucks that it’s him” confession is a **late-arc unlock**, earned only as the wall fails. Don’t spend it early. (This guard and her withheld lushness are the same wall doing the same job.)

## Show, don’t tell — the dominant narrative stance (LOCKED)

**This governs every scene, and it outranks the confiding-narrator texture and the banter dial wherever they ever pull against it.**

The reader works out what’s happening — and what it *means* — from what the characters do, say, and notice. **Never from the narrator stepping outside the scene to explain it.** Show the thing clearly enough that the reader arrives at the meaning on their own; that arrival belongs to the reader.

Cut, on sight:
- **Significance-flagging.** “This is the part that mattered.” “For the record.” “Here’s the thing.” Any line whose job is to tell the reader how to weigh a moment.
- **Self-narration of arc.** A character naming their own wound, growth, competence, or change (“I was finally useful,” “this was the morning I stopped being afraid”). Show the act; let the conclusion be the reader’s.
- **Explaining a feeling after it has already landed.** If the body and the action carry it, deleting the explanation makes it stronger.
- **Do-the-thing-then-gloss-the-moral** — a punchy closing line that names what the scene meant (“No technique. Just truth.”). End on the shown thing, not the moral. This applies inside Do-yun’s lush channel too: lush *texture*, never lush *editorializing*.

**The test:** strike every line where the narrator addresses the reader to explain meaning, flag importance, or name their own significance. If the scene still works, it was doing its job. If the meaning evaporates, fix the *showing* — don’t restore the telling.

## Voice constitution

1. **Dialogue carries the surface.** Long, fast, tag-light volleys tracked by rhythm; the verbal spar *is* the flirtation. But on a charged beat the talk goes quiet and the interiority/restraint carries — dialogue beating thought is only true between the charged beats, never on them.
1. **Interiority is load-bearing and POV-channeled — not uniformly low.** Do-yun pours (lush, on charged beats; plain between). Jae withholds (dry, fast, short-burst; her lush line is a late unlock). Between the charged beats, momentum and banter carry the room.
1. **Name the feeling, then undercut it** — one sincere beat, then a deflection. The naming is the *character’s* own fast in-character flicker before the deflection; it is never the narrator explaining to the reader what the moment means. *Show, don’t tell* outranks.
1. **The narrator confides, but never explains** — direct address and the confiding aside are part of the voice (narrator and reader as co-conspirators against the room), but for **comedy, intimacy, and deflection only — never to explain significance or hand the reader a conclusion.**
1. **Comedy is the surface; the slow-burn is the substance.** The banter dial is up (dialogue / Do-yun’s surface / ensemble), but comedy *surrounds* the ache and goes silent for it — it never lands on the charged beat and never displaces the interiority. No scene is wall-to-wall ache; heaviness lands because it’s framed by light.
1. **The world talks** — the ensemble (KAIROS, the Jeongs, Pak, Halmeoni, the diner, the agency) is a comic chorus that heckles, escalates, and outs feelings the leads won’t admit; plot smuggled under the back-and-forth; every secondary voice instantly recognizable.
1. **MBTI underneath, never named.**
1. **Logic is load-bearing** — every beat survives a skeptical reread; track canon in the state log, and ripple any new fact into BIBLE + INDEX the same turn.
