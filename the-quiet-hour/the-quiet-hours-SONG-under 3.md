# “UNDER” — Do-yun’s unfinished solo *(the rain song · the one he sings in the dark)*

*His private, self-written song — “moves only when she’s near, freezes when she’s gone; she is its missing end.” Started the week of the convenience-store night, three years ago. **UNFINISHED by design:** it dies on the cliff (“And the light had a—”) and has never once gone past it. **Unplugged: one male voice, one acoustic guitar (he keeps tempo on the low string — the rhythm motif), no production.** The deliberate sonic inverse of “Right on Time” (product vs. person, same man). Lyrics kept oblique — the* who *stays hidden (per Ch2: “the meaning stays in his mouth”).*

> **TWO ON-PAGE MOMENTS — keep distinct:**
> 
> 1. **The open-window take (Ch18) = the LEAK SOURCE.** Three days out, undone by overhearing Jae keep his discarded scraps in the back of her ledger, Do-yun opens the window for air (not to hush himself — he sings all day, she’s a night creature and awake; being heard costs him nothing) and, for the first and only time, lets the *true* song out **with its meaning showing** — sung openly to the dark city, guitar and all. He sings it up to the cliff and stops as always (outro still missing). **A neighbour across the gap films the backlit silhouette + voice + guitar; face obscured; he never knows.** That capture is the clip that detonates in Ch20. Jae hears it through the wall the same night (Ch17), undone, with no idea what it is or that it’s about to be famous (subliminal-exposure engine).
> 1. **The resolution (later, to write) = a with-her moment, indoors.** The wordless lift that finally carries it *past* the cliff only opens when she’s actually near — neither of them knowing she’s the reason. The completed outro is the late-book payoff (lands ~Ch32). Stage it **indoors** (present ≈ February — the old fire-escape “balcony” version, `ch09-balcony.md`, is **retired** for that reason; its recognition beat is folded into Ch17). **Don’t spend the outro at the leak.**

**Leave the ending broken.** The `[Outro]` cuts off on “And the light had a” on purpose — the cliff it never gets past. If the model tries to resolve it, let the melody lift and finish **wordlessly** (hums/oohs), not in words. Completing that line is a late-book payoff; don’t spend it here.
-----

**The missing end is already on the page — encoded.** The Ch3 napkin (Jae’s POV) and Ch4 (Do-yun writing it) both carry the outro’s answer in his private shorthand: **“gold, no word.”** *Gold* is what completes “And the light had a—”: her eyes, green-going-to-gold — the light in the window that *had a gold to it.* *No word* is this exact rule — it resolves **wordlessly**, the meaning stays in his mouth; and literally, he has **no word for it** (doesn’t know “heterochromia,” per story-state-log: “green going to gold… He has no word for it”). He has the ending and forbids himself the word for it.

Jae finds the one scrap he never stripped the lyric off (dropped in the move-in chaos) and reads it as nonsense — **firewall-safe, a banked recognition detonator:** she is holding the answer to his three-years-unfinished song, about her own eyes, and can’t read it. **Napkin order = “gold, no word”** (Ch3 = reference; Ch4 matched word-for-word). Pairs with **Ch8**, where Jae *hums* “the end… the part I’ve been missing for three years” without knowing she carries it — his muse-feeling; the structural resolution still lands **~Ch32**, wordless, indoors, with her near.
