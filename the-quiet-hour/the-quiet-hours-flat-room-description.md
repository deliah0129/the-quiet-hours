# THE QUIET HOURS — Room Description Reference Sheet

*Jae-wu’s flat. Sensory and physical description pulled from the entry passage, organized for reuse in drafting. These are the established, canon-consistent details — reach for them so the space stays consistent across chapters. Phrasings are starting points, not locked prose.*

-----

## THE APPROACH (getting in)

- Old walk-up; **no lift, four flights.**
- The **stairwell timer light** dies on the third landing — fails the same way every night.
- The climb she takes by memory: **eleven steps, a half-step landing, eleven more.**
- The lock her key knows before her hand does.

## THE BOLT (hers)

- **Brass slide-bolt**, stiff. The one thing in the flat that is hers and answers only to her.
- Seats with a sound **like a small full stop.**
- A **second, inside bolt** on her bedroom door.

## THE GENKAN / ENTRY

- Step-down entry; **shoes off, set straight against the wall.**
- Now a **second pair** there too — men’s, dark, toes squared to the baseboard the way she leaves hers.

## AIR / TEMPERATURE

- The flat holds **the cold of a place no one has heated** (she works nights — it sits unheated for ~7 hours).

## THE SOUNDS (her ledger)

The list she can name with her eyes shut and counts herself down on:

- **Refrigerator hum**
- **Tick of the wall clock**
- A **pipe**
- The **bus on the avenue**
- The **building settling on its old joints**
- *(her cadence: “fridge, clock, pipe, the bus on the avenue, nothing, nothing, mine.”)*
- **The one sound too many:** through the wall, someone **breathing in sleep — lower than the fridge.**

## THE THIN WALL (the intimacy engine)

- **Bad plaster** between her room and the other.
- The distance across it: **six feet, maybe five** — “a hand’s-width and a wall between.”
- Beds **head to head** on either side of it.

## LIVING / KITCHEN

- The **mustard couch** — a hump of deeper black against the window’s grey at night.
- The **low table** (where they eat): two washed **bowls stacked, his on hers.**
- The **galley** kitchen: smells of something **scorched, then scrubbed,** aired out through **the one window that opens.**
- The **tap** runs **from rust to clear** before it’s drinkable.
- The **dish rack:** the glass joins the two bowls — **four pieces of crockery together.**
- She **eats and drinks standing up** — “the way she does everything that doesn’t require a chair.”

## SMELL

- Under the scorched-and-scrubbed kitchen air: **vetiver and cedar** — “cold and green and expensive, in a flat where nothing is.”
- It’s **in her tea towels.** (Do-yun’s scent intruding on her space — an irritant to her, not yet an admission.)

## HER ROOM

- **Narrow, square, hers.**
- **Bed against the plaster** (the shared wall).
- The **inside bolt** clicks — “a call and its echo” to the breathing through the wall.

## THE FRAME (the whole place)

- **“A flat built for one and a half.”**
- Two people now, “both pretending the wall is thicker than it is.”

### CONSISTENCY NOTES

- **No-lift, 4th-floor walk-up** — keep the climb a small recurring labor.
- The **brass bolt** is *her* motif (autonomy / a door that locks); don’t give it to anyone else.
- The **thin shared wall** carries household sound (kettle, shower-hum, breathing); a low voice through it = unintelligible murmur. Keep distances small (“five, six feet”).
- **Vetiver & cedar** is Do-yun’s fixed scent — when it appears in her space, it reads as intrusion to her, never admiration (canon: her POV must not leak tender admiration).
- She **counts to self-regulate**; near him the count **scatters** — use sparingly so it stays a real beat.
