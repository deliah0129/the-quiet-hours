# THE QUIET HOURS — Sensory Bible: The Three Rooms

> **What this is.** Walk-into texture you draft *from* — the place built sense by sense so a scene remembers the room instead of inventing it fresh. **Companion to `flat-room-description.md`, not a replacement** (that sheet already holds the flat’s physical inventory — don’t duplicate it). This adds the two things that sheet lacks: the **register-split overlay** (every room rendered through *both* eyes), and the **two ungathered rooms** (the galbi house, the cold city).
> 
> **The world’s whole argument is one contrast:** the **priced city** (cold, glass, ledgered) vs. the **unpriced kitchen** (warm, fed-for-free) — and the **flat suspended between them**, where the quiet hours happen. Build every room to serve that.
> 
> **Split-aware throughout.** Jae’s dry eye **catalogues** a room (noted, audited, held at arm’s length — and near him the count *scatters*). Do-yun’s lush eye lets the same room **pour** (warmth, texture, the place as relief or as threat). Same walls, two registers. Phrasings are starting points, never locked prose. Nothing here surfaces as description-for-its-own-sake — it’s the iceberg; spend an inch.

## ROOM 1 — THE FLAT (the suspended space)

*Physical inventory lives in `flat-room-description.md`. This is only the register overlay it’s missing.*

**Through Jae’s dry eye (catalogued — armor):**
The flat is a list she can run with her eyes shut: fridge, clock, pipe, bus, the building on its joints — *nothing, nothing, mine.* Things are counted, not felt. The cold is a fact (seven unheated hours), not a hardship. His second pair of shoes, toes squared to the baseboard the way she leaves hers, is *noted* — an entry, a variable with no column. The vetiver-cedar in her tea towels is an **intrusion**, an expensive smell trespassing on a flat where nothing is, filed under *spy* and irritation — **never** admiration. The bolt seating like a full stop is the one line in the ledger that balances. Her register here is short, dry, audited. When the count scatters near him, that’s the wall failing — ration it; it’s a real beat, not wallpaper.

**Through Do-yun’s lush eye (pours — relief):**
The same flat is the first unmonitored air he’s breathed in fifteen years. The cold isn’t a fact; it’s *hers* — a place that smells of scorched-then-scrubbed rice and her, not stage makeup and ozone. The bad plaster he’s grateful for: her breathing on the other side, lower than the fridge, is the metronome he can finally sleep to. The mustard couch, the four pieces of crockery drying together (his bowl on hers), the one window that opens — he reads them as *texture*, the grammar of a real life, and lets them land. **Guardrail:** his pour here is warmth-and-relief, not possession; and the firewall holds — he knows store=diner=roommate, never party-girl. His lush channel blooms on the charged beats (her hand, the held tap, the song moving through the wall) and goes plain between.

## ROOM 2 — MAEIL GALBI (the warm pole)

*The Jeong family’s galbi house (Reo / Joon’s family). Jae does the books here from Ch12. The refuser home — feeds her “till she dies,” no transaction. Gather from Ch22; extend in that grain.*

**The fixed inventory (canon + extension):**

- **Time that matters:** two in the afternoon — “the best room in the city and nobody knows it but the people who can’t leave.” Lunch rush thinned to one soju table up front; the kitchen dropped into its **low hour** — eating standing up, fighting quietly, resetting before dinner.
- **Sound:** the **hood fan droning.** The **rice warmer breathing** in the corner. The **side fridge holding its one flat sour note** — the wrong one, the one she told them to call a man about a month ago and they didn’t. (That sour note is hers to keep: a discrepancy only the auditor hears, in the one place she’s safe.)
- **Smell:** seared galbi over everything; under it, the rice warmer’s starch-warmth; scrubbed steel.
- **Surfaces:** the **steel counter** where the ledger lives, borrowed pencil going. Cold steel under her forearms — the galbi-house twin of the kitchen counter in the flat.
- **The people as fixtures:** **Eomma** across the kitchen, arguing with a delivery slip; sharp; sorts people into guest-vs-owed-it; sets a plate of trim at Jae’s elbow *without asking, because in this house not eating is a thing that gets noticed and corrected.* **Min-ji** out front, pretending the soju table can’t see her watching the clock. The **halmeoni** in the **warm seam beside the rice warmer**, tailing a bowl of beansprouts one at a time with her thumbs — “a small soft fixture I’d decided months ago had simply always been there and always would be, the way the stove is there.”
- **Extension (overwritable):** the stool Jae’s claimed — left side, back to the wall, sightline to the door (the auditor’s seat; she always knows the exits, the dry-eye twin of his exit-mapping). The banchan fridge’s rubber seal. The ticket spike. The cold that lives by the door and never reaches the rice-warmer seam.

**Through Jae’s dry eye:**
This is the one room where her register *loosens without breaking* — not lush, but unguarded. Numbers behave here; the books are honest; work doesn’t congratulate her and then ask for something back. She catalogues the warmth the way she catalogues everything, but she doesn’t audit it for the bill, because she’s already worked out the bill is zero. The plate set down without asking is the closest thing to being chosen she lets herself receive. Her dryness here reads as *home*, not armor — the difference is everything.

**Through Do-yun’s lush eye (if he’s ever here — currently he came “once, as no one,” Ch17):**
The warm pole would *flood* his channel — fed-for-free is the exact thing the machine has no column for, the thing he’s starved of. Handle with the firewall: Reo compartmentalizes the restaurant from idol life; the band is never a topic at the till. If he’s rendered in this room, it’s the unpriced kitchen overwhelming a man who’s only ever been priced.

## ROOM 3 — THE COLD CITY / THE WALK BETWEEN (the glass pole)

*Present ≈ February. Mapo (Ch21 realign: Mangwon → Mapo). Working-class walk-up Korea, not the idol-world glass — the priced city Jae lives adjacent to but not inside. The blankest room; build it.*

**The fixed inventory (build, overwritable):**

- **Season/light:** February. Cold that gets into the hands. Light gone by five; the sodium-orange of streetlamps on wet pavement; the blue-grey of late afternoon on steel and shutter.
- **The walk:** flat to galbi, **eleven minutes at her pace** — kept steady (the steadiness is the character; she controls what she can). Narrow Mapo backstreets; the avenue with its **bus** (the same bus in her sound-ledger). Shuttered storefronts, a convenience-store glow, the hiss of a tarp, exhaust-and-cold air.
- **The cold as a moral fact:** the city doesn’t heat itself for her any more than the flat does. Warmth in this world is *bought* (the galbi house buys it with a fire and a family; the idol world buys it with money). The street between is where neither warmth reaches — the suspended cold she crosses twice a day.
- **The priced world at the edges (substrate, mostly off-page):** the glass towers she doesn’t enter; the ad with a face on it (his face advertises the maison he wears); the merger world that prices people. Jae moves *under* it, in the part the machine doesn’t bother to price — which is why it erased her twice. The reform/scarcity machinery (the CPA apprenticeship bottleneck) is this same cold, institutional, indifferent.

**Through Jae’s dry eye:**
The cold is logistics — eleven minutes, steady pace, hands in pockets, bolt at the end. She doesn’t romanticize the street; she crosses it. The priced city is *weather happening to other people* — “a thing that did not bill me and so did not concern me” (her literal Ch22 stance on fame). Her dryness about the glass world is the audit eye refusing to be impressed by what it can’t trust.

**Through Do-yun’s lush eye:**
The same cold city is where he’s *hunted* — exits, phones, the camera that could end him. His channel reads the street as threat-texture (the too-long look, the raised phone) and the flat at the end of it as the only door that doesn’t open onto the machine. The February cold on his hands is the un-makeup’d, un-managed world he fled into and can finally feel.

## THE THREE ROOMS AS ONE ARGUMENT (don’t lose this)

- **Galbi house** = warmth given for free (the unpriced kitchen; the HEA’s home). Jae’s register *loosens* here.
- **Cold city** = warmth withheld / warmth priced (the glass pole; the machine’s indifference). Both registers go *guarded* here — hers to logistics, his to threat.
- **The flat** = the suspended space where the two pressures meet, and where, in the quiet hours, an unpriced warmth gets made between two people who were each discarded by the cold one. Her register *audits* it; his *pours* into it; the slow burn is the gap closing.

The booth, the ledger, the steel counters, the bolt, the bus, the rice warmer’s breath — these are the visible inches. Everything researched this session is the ice beneath them. Spend the inch; leave the rest under the water.
