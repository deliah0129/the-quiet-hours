# THE LEADS

## Jae — Yun Jae-wu (윤재우) // born Baek Sori (백소리, *secret*) · ~24 · **INFJ (Ni-Fe-Ti-Se)**

**Who she is.** The leaseholder. Final-year accounting student, top of her class, unhireable without the licence — so she pours coffee and waits tables and now keeps the books for a galbi house, funding the last of the degree. Broke, fiercely self-sufficient, face-blind by training. To her, her roommate is only **“Doha,”** a soft man hiding from a family; her private working theory is that he’s a spy. Narration is hers on the odd chapters.

**The engine (INFJ, locked).**

- **Ni (dominant):** reads the *essence* of a person and skips the facts — clocks the drowning behind his grey eyes in Ch1 without knowing a thing about him; in Ch17 hears him *building* the song through the wall and knows the wound by heart. One deep current, never a list.
- **Fe (auxiliary) — the one-way valve:** care runs strictly outward (the comped coffee, the fish-stall cover, feeding herself last). She has no procedure for being given to — the shimmed table (Ch5), the no-transaction tea (Ch7) short-circuit her. Her wound lives here: *who would want me.* Heal = letting the valve run inward.
- **Ti (tertiary):** the number, the till to the won, the books that must balance. Numbers are armor — and near him they start to fail (Ch5 “the numbers had refused her”; Ch17 the locks “counted twice, stayed three” and still don’t hold).
- **Se (inferior):** the want only ever shows up as sensation she won’t name and reframes as a hazard (the towel in Ch3, the held tap in Ch7). Under max stress she grips Se — flag for the verge and dark moment.

**Voice on the page.** Dry, terse aloud; the comedy is the deadpan gap between what she sees and what she’ll say. She **clocks, she doesn’t quip** — the verbal engine is his. Confessions-to-the-reader stay in **suspicion / irritation, never admiration** (canon-protected through the mid-arc). Counting is her register; never give her his beats-vocabulary.

**Name layers (wound in miniature).** Born **Baek Sori** (소리, “sound”) → erased as a toddler → renamed **Yun Jae-wu** (在宇, “you exist, the world has a place for you”) by the group-home woman who claimed her → goes by **Jae** (在, only “to be here,” never claimed the 宇). Reveal restores Sori — reuniting the musician’s muse with the word for music. “Yun Jae-wu” reads male on a lease (the disguise mechanic).

**Arc as drafted (Ch01–21).** Origin kindness (Ch1) → the move-in collision and the eyes that snag (Ch3) → cohabitation, the spy tally, armor cracking (Ch5/7) → her world: Director Oh’s precarity, the market, Han-byeol (Ch9/11) → being *heard* at last via the bookkeeping fix at Reo’s family restaurant (Ch12/13, dignity not charity) → closes the accounts on Oh, the agency, the warehouse (Ch15/17) → **Ji-hwan reopens** (Ch17) → exam morning (Ch19/20) → the quiet thing comes apart (Ch20–21, off her page; she’s the last to know).

## Do-yun — Kang Do-yun (강도윤) // idol **“Seungwoo”** (승우) // alias **“Doha”** · 26 · **ENFJ (Fe-Ni-Se-Ti)**

**Who he is.** K-pop main vocal / visual / center of the self-producing group **KAIROS**, *and* the blood heir of the Kang family’s luxury house **SEORÉ** — a firewall the public doesn’t know. Burnt out, hiding off-grid in Jae’s box room under the plain name “Doha.” His goal isn’t love; it’s **two stolen months as a person, not a product** — to breathe, to finish the song. Narration is his on the even chapters (plus Ch20–21).

**The engine (ENFJ — Jae’s stack inverted; do NOT relabel to INFJ).**

- **Fe (dominant) — the gift the machine harvests:** reading and moving people is literally his job; the idol smile is this function strip-mined for product. *Wanted as a product, never chosen as a person* guts him specifically. The coffee detonates because it’s the first thing handed to him with nothing wanted back.
- **Ni (auxiliary):** the one fixed point — once it locks onto her it won’t release. Enacted as the drumming↔still tell and the orbit; the song moves when she’s near, freezes when she’s gone. Never named.
- **Se (tertiary):** the rich, immediate sensory voice — cloth, the maple, the rain, the song-with-rain-in-it. He lives easily in the body she can’t feel safe in, which is *why he* can meet her in sensation.
- **Ti (inferior) — the cover stories:** “vocal rest,” “it suits me,” “strategic.” Flag every rationalization; it’s almost always Fe-caretaking in disguise — and the seed of the dark-moment over-managing. (Note: the rigging is **cut** as a plot device; the dark moment is now the party-girl surfacing. But inferior-Ti cover stories still run.)

**Three names = three selves = the wound.** Seungwoo (the product), Kang Do-yun (the bloodline), Doha (the nobody he’s trying to become). Storm-grey eyes are the one thing he can’t dye out — the recognition motif. Idol ash-silver, **self-box-dyed black** to vanish (Ch4); the machine silvers him back at 7 a.m. on engagement day (Ch20). Counts in **beats** (musician). Genuinely shy with women; she’s the single exception who flips him to rogue, because her reactions are to *him.* Tells: fingers drum when dysregulated, still when regulated; split knuckles (the party). Cartier ambassador (the watch source; shed by Ch7).

**Voice on the page.** The **extrovert engine** — fast, charming, fills silence, processes outward; his chapters carry the most banter (charm is his trained surface). Longing shows in how relentlessly he attends to her. In Ch20–21 the freeze becomes the spine: *“I am the verbal one… and the word for it would not come.”*

**Arc as drafted (Ch01–21).** The booth / vocal-rest wound and the diner re-finding (Ch2) → the collision from his side (Ch4) → the machine reclaims him: the fitting, the mother, the panic, the phone-off flight home (Ch6) → learning to be broke; the market; the rent fix at Reo’s (Ch8/10/12) → the engagement clock tightens, the Yuna hour (Ch10/14/16) → the open-window take that becomes the leak (Ch18) → **Day-Zero:** the clip detonates, label vs. mother split, his **first self-made choice** (“the song stays mine”), the engagement smile (Ch20) → the band does the math, the freeze takes the last second (Ch21).

# DO-YUN’S WORLD

## Hyun — manager & de facto older brother · ~38

**Who he is.** A never-debuted trainee who aged out without ever reaching the stage and was kept on to mind the kids who would (halved his one meal). Knows the machine because it used him up and never chose him: *I didn’t get picked, you did, I won’t let it break you.* **“Have you eaten”** is his and Do-yun’s code. Reached Do-yun first after the party; greenlit the sublet because the lease name read male; **the only one who knows the flat.** Appears most of the book (Ch2,3,4,6,7,8,10,12,14,16,18,20,21) — driver, fixer, brother. Ch20: bursts in without knocking (first time in fifteen years) to handle the leak; quietly hands Yuna a glass of water no one asked for — the seed of **Yuna × Hyun**, the parallel romance (the never-chosen one finally chosen). His arc rhymes with both leads: *used → chosen.*

## KAIROS — the band (chosen family of 15 years; group text = lifeline)

Self-producing five; Do-yun = main vocal / visual / center. They function as the comic chorus and the made-family that “does the math” no machine can. All four arrive in person Ch20–21.

### Tae — leader · steady, low-volume authority

The one who can stop a room without raising his voice (“both of you” — and they stop). Checks Do-yun for damage head-to-foot and says nothing, “which from Tae is a paragraph.” Lets *“fine”* sit “the way he’s let it sit for fifteen years.” In Ch20/21 he’s the one who names the one wall the band can’t stand in front of: *“You have to be the one to tell her.”* Costly, quiet loyalty. **Introverted-feeling-led leader register (e.g. INFJ/ISFJ); the calm to Sun’s noise.**

### Reo — rapper · blunt, fiercest defender, secretly the cook

Can’t stop moving (turns coals that don’t need turning); freezes labelled meal-prep tubs. His family runs the **Jeong / Maeil Galbi house** where Jae now keeps the books — the thread that makes him **the one who connects the dots** (Ch21: “the accountant who does my mother’s books is the woman you’ve been hiding with”). Reads a room in four seconds. Blunt to the bone but drops the armor for the truths that matter (*“You absolute disaster… the country found your voice before you found your nerve”*). Lower-profile than Do-yun (rapper, not the face) + family cover = celebrity-plausible at the restaurant. Appears Ch4,6,8,10,12,15,16,17,18,20,21.

### Sun — maknae · the heart · **EXTROVERT (ESFP) — LOCKED**

**The deliberate opposite of Yejun.** Warm, loud, emotionally outward, present-tense, can’t *not* react — feelings arrive on the surface and out the mouth immediately. On the page: cries at soup adverts; in the Ch20 group text he’s all-caps and instant (*“HYUNG is that YOU… i’d know that voice in my SLEEP,” “i’m CRYING in a CARPARK and also WHO IS IT ABOUT”*); reads the love-song before anyone (“the room is a love song hyung wrote a LOVE SONG”); the sound he makes is “a kettle reaching the boil.” He’s the one who says the unsayable thing out loud (*“He didn’t tell her because he likes her”*). Comedy/warmth engine. Appears Ch2,4,6,8,10,18,20,21.

- *Typing note:* ESFP (Se-Fi-Te-Ni) gives the loud, present, emotionally-expressive maknae — outward-processing, the heart on the sleeve. The contrast with Yejun (inward, dry, analytical) is the band’s comic polarity; keep it sharp.

### Yejun — the dry strategist · product-perfect cold foil

The introvert counterweight to Sun. Inward, analytical, clipped, *“never unkind — he’s just accurate, which is worse.”* Secretly tracks every member’s family birthdays (files his own dentist-coded so he won’t dodge it) — hidden care under the cold. In Ch20 he’s the one who clocks the recording is *“somewhere small and soft. Somewhere with curtains”* — four seconds from the secret. Does the timestamp math overnight in Ch21. **Introverted-thinking-forward register (ISTJ/INTJ-adjacent); the still water to Sun’s spill.** Appears Ch4,6,8,10,18,20,21.

## The Mother — Kang matriarch (married-in widow)

A poor girl who married into the Kang line for security, not love; the in-laws cut the father off for marrying beneath him; he died young, leaving her a poor, rejected widow with the blood-heir infant. She clawed back by making the baby **undeniable and bankable** (commercials from infancy, idol at twelve). She **manages, never mothers** — he’s her asset, her cash cow, her citizenship. The merger locks her place forever — she’s not nervous, she’s **desperate.** Kept human by the clawed-up terror of falling back to nothing; **never admits** she takes his choices (frames it as *sparing* him the burden). On the page she never enters rooms directly post-Ch6 — she works by reach (Ch20: a word in the coordinator’s ear drops the temperature of the hall). Her order on Day-Zero — *bury it* — collides with the label’s *sell it*, and that collision hands Do-yun his first choice. Voice: talks to the mirror, reads people like cloth, “a coat doesn’t get to decide it’d rather be a curtain.” Dramatic-irony anchor: her “the girl with the tray” (Ch6) is Jae, behind his wall, unknown to her. She becomes the villain of the dark moment (weaponizing the party-girl truth). Appears Ch2,6,(10 corridor).

**TYPE / MIRROR / FATE — LOCKED 2026-06-09.** **ENTJ (Te-Ni-Se-Fi)** — NOT Fe, NOT INFJ (she overrides feeling, never tends it). Te-dom empire-architect (“made the baby bankable”; works by lever, not attunement); Ni long-forecast (read the 25-yr arc off an infant); Se reads the live room; **inferior Fi** = the blind spot — she cannot perceive love-for-its-own-sake, so a freely-chosen no-price bond reads to her as “not real” and she can neither parse nor counter it. **Mirror with Jae = SAME WOUND, OPPOSITE WIRING** (Jae INFJ / mother ENTJ): same can’t-receive fortress off the same terror of being discarded — **Jae goes small/invisible (inward), the mother goes huge/indispensable (outward).** She is who Jae becomes if the fear wins; Jae beats her by receiving freely. She shares Do-yun’s Ni but her dom **Te** vs his **Fe** is why she predicts him perfectly and never reaches him. **FATE = redeemable (“she can bear it”):** stripped of titles/assets, forced into dependency on the family she owned (small allowance; room/place TBD) — the punishment *is* the cure (sentenced to the dependency she always feared = the gift her Fi could never take). Power was borrowed (regent of the sole blood heir; senior Kangs dead → also why no one contests her AND why she’s Do-yun’s last blood); his autonomy evaporates it; he keeps her *for free, because family.* See ARCHITECTURE-LOCKED §ENDGAME (2026-06-09).

## Director Ahn — SEORÉ atelier head

Has fitted Do-yun since he was seventeen (“the cloth can’t hang off a boy who’s folding”); one of the very few who reads him true; warm and dry. Catches the amateur home-dye from visible evidence alone (won’t claim to know *why*). Won’t make him stop the finger-count, just “not into her pins.” Books the colourist to re-silver him for the 14th. Parting line: *“keep some of it — not the hair, the rest.”* Craft-stakes secondary; no romance function. Appears Ch6,8,10,11,14,18.

## Noh — label head

Wants the reverse of the mother: keep Do-yun performing, deliver the comeback (the label owns KAIROS; the comeback is theirs to sell). Day-Zero he wants to **sell** the leak — *“this is the comeback… the heir’s secret heartbreak, you cannot buy this.”* The predatory-label turn goes live post-viral (blacklist leverage). Mostly off-page pressure (Ch2,6,8,18,20).

## Seo Yuna — the merger bride · reluctant ally, NOT a rival

Finds Do-yun attractive but doesn’t want the marriage and gains nothing from it (her family’s already rich). **Fellow prisoner, sardonic gallows humor, glamour-as-armor.** Reads Do-yun cold and kind in one move (Ch10: *“You’ve got one too… keep it off your face in your mother’s weather”*) — the most useful thing anyone says to him in a year, from the woman picked to be his wife. Ch20, on the engagement step, lips not moving: *“Some mergers are funerals. Yours has a soundtrack now… You just made yourself expensive. Expensive things are harder to throw away.”* Has a jealous boyfriend (**Min-gyu**) — the wildcard. When the Jae marriage kills the merger, Yuna is freed and **chooses Hyun.** Appears Ch10,14,16,18,20.

## Min-gyu — Yuna’s boyfriend · jilted antagonist (idol-side leak)

Jealous, doesn’t want her marrying an idol; “writes things down” — the **merger/wedding whisper account** (seeded Ch10, advanced Ch18). Distinct from the viral song leak (that’s the anonymous neighbour through the open window). In Ch20 his account is named as one of the suspected origins of the spread. Loses Yuna when the merger dies; the jilted-antagonist thread (subplot C/D) braids into the Yuna × Hyun resolution. Appears Ch18,20.

# JAE’S WORLD

## Ji-hwan — Jae’s ex · antagonist, thread **X**

**Who he is.** Two years together; she ended it because she caught him lying smoothly (a false bottom in a person, the way she reads one in a number). He cried, was good at it, and **never accepted the *no*** — treats her silence as a clerical error that’ll correct itself if he waits by the door long enough, smiling. Introduced **Ch17** in the flesh: turns up at Maeil Galbi on a rumour, all wattage, and in a warm half-second Eomma lets slip the name **“Doha”** — which he files. By Ch17’s close he texts her the building she lives over (*“the place over the laundromat”*) — information no one gave him, the tell that he doesn’t stop. Knows “Doha” and the address. **Double duty (per the locked back-half chain):** outs Jae specifically *and* triggers the close-call that lands her in hospital. Reappears Ch19; escalation and close-call (Ch24–25) are outlined, not yet drafted. Voice: easy, sorry-not-sorry warmth, “takes the thing you meant as a wall and treats it as a door.”

## Director Oh — predatory gig-boss

Texture of her precarity: skims pay, dangles worse shifts, the cold-storage/warehouse leverage. By Ch17 he’s a **closed account** — she’s cut the line; he texts trying to reopen it (*“Cold storage’s still open if you’ve come to your senses”*) and she deletes it clean. He’s the off-ramp she’s already walked: the bookkeeping at the restaurant freed her. Function: shows her learning to close doors and have the world not end (the rhyme Ji-hwan then violates). Slated to close ~Ch15 region. Appears across her chapters as pressure (Ch2/3/5/7/9/11/15/17/19 references).

## Han-byeol — Jae’s *chosen* roommate

Left for Vancouver (a real program); her leaving is what forced the sight-unseen sublet — the dorm irony (Jae engineered her whole life to avoid random-stranger roommates and landed in exactly that). The chosen-family ache: in Ch9 the Vancouver call lands as being *thought of.* Appears Ch3,5,9,13.

## Eomma — Reo’s mother · Maeil Galbi matriarch (Jeongneung)

Restaurant owner drowning in a shoebox of receipts and a pink-paper tax letter; sharp, warm, brusque (“I cook for liars” — a drowning owner testing a stranger). Takes to Jae instantly; feeds her free “till she dies”; deliberately does the gas man wrong “to see if you’d catch it.” Knows Jae only because Reo phoned ahead — **no mystery, no arrangement, not related to Jae** (and explicitly separate from the secret heir-grandmother). In Ch17 she reads Ji-hwan as a liar in two fingers and bins his cup; lays out, over receipts, the whole difference between a man who reads a room as a guest and one who reads it as a thing he’s owed. The warm room that will be the loudest about Do-yun by lunch (Ch21). Appears Ch12,17 (in person); restaurant thread seeded Ch6/8/10.

# THE HEIR FAMILY *(SECRET — seed-only; never surface in prose until the reveal)*

Per the locked back-half chain, the heir reveal fires late (donor-seed at hospital intake → DNA → grandmother live), **after** the idol reveal. Seeds on the page so far = her heterochromia (the bloodline trait), the blank-trace toddler origin, the name’s “you have a place” irony. Add no overt foreshadowing.

- **The half-sister** — innocent (a baby too), raised as the only daughter / presumed heir; **lacks the full heterochromia** (so Jae’s eyes are the proof). The reveal becomes a relationship and a shared tragedy, not a rivalry.
