# THE QUIET HOURS — Creative Wordlist

A working palette, not a checklist. Pull from it; don’t pour from it.

**Register note.** Most of this is fuel for **Do-yun’s lush channel** — rationed, on charged beats, plain between. **Jae’s channel withholds**: she gets the concrete nouns and the audit-eye words, almost never the luminous ones (until the late-arc unlock). And every word here still has to serve show-don’t-tell — texture, never editorializing. A gorgeous word that explains the moment is worse than a plain one that shows it.

## 3. Chapter “rule” title words

*Your titles are rules — joke on the surface, thesis underneath. The richest seam is **Jae’s own vocabulary**: accounting and contract terms that double as the emotional truth of the chapter. She’d title her life this way.*

### Accounting / contract words that double as emotional rules

*(literal meaning / what the chapter is really about)*

- **Materiality** — only disclose what’s material *(she’s deciding what counts)*
- **Going Concern** — assume the entity survives *(will this last)*
- **Liability** — what you owe *(what he is to her)*
- **Provision** — money set aside for a loss you expect *(bracing for him to leave)*
- **Impairment** — an asset worth less than its book value *(the wall, failing)*
- **Reconcile** — make two records agree *(obvious)*
- **Accrue** — it builds up whether or not it’s been paid *(feeling, compounding)*
- **Write-Off** — the loss you stop pretending to recover *(letting go)*
- **Disclosure** — the note you’re legally required to make *(the confession)*
- **Audit** — someone checks what you actually did *(being seen)*
- **Depreciation** — value lost just by time passing
- **Amortize** — spread the cost over the life of it
- **Liquidate** — turn it to cash; dissolve it
- **Default** — failure to meet the terms
- **In Arrears** — behind on what’s due
- **Net** — what’s left after everything’s taken out
- **Balance** — the two sides have to match
- **Contingent** — depends on something that may not happen
- **Tenant in Common** — co-ownership, undivided *(the lease, the room, them)*

### Plain “rule” words (proximity / domestic / contract)

Proximity, Sublet, Threshold, Tenancy, Containment, Custody, Overlap, Quiet Enjoyment (a real lease clause — perfect), Notice, Forwarding Address, Shared Wall, House Rules, Lights Out, Do Not Disturb, Occupancy, Lease Term, Sub-Letter, Common Area, Quiet Hours
