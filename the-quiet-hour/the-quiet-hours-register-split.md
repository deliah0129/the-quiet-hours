> **One-line version:** One house style, split by POV — **Do-yun pours, Jae withholds** — and the split *is* the why-them, rendered in prose.

-----

## The two channels

**Do-yun (even chapters) — the lush channel.**
Atmospheric stacking, sensory triads, the held-and-released breath. Not decoration — his tertiary **Se** wiring on the page: the trained body that reads every room in texture (cloth-by-touch, maple, rain). His prose *pours*. The lush cadence the research kept reaching for is, correctly aimed, **his native voice — and only his.**

**Jae (odd chapters) — the dry channel.**
Concrete nouns, short bursts, the feeling turned over for the bill before it’s allowed to land. The audit eye. Her lushness is **withheld by default.** That withholding is the slow-burn engine: when the wall finally fails and she lets *one* image bloom, it detonates precisely because she spent twenty chapters refusing to spend it. Her rare lush line is a confession the dry lines make possible.

-----

## Why it’s structural, not stylistic

Before, “literary lush vs. dry restraint” read like an unresolved fight between two house-style docs. The split resolves it: it’s **one** style with a reason — his function stack vs. hers, the same four cognitive functions with the top two inverted (Jae INFJ / Do-yun ENFJ). The prose becomes the **why-them.** The reader feels the difference between the leads before they can name it.

-----

## Guardrails (hold these or the split smears back to mush)

1. **His lush is rationed even inside his own chapters.** Wall-to-wall stacking goes numb and reverts to the AI-essay cadence. It blooms on charged beats (the booth, her hand, the song moving) and pulls back to plain between them. Lush as **spotlight, not wallpaper** — the contrast lives inside his chapters too, not only across the POV line.
1. **The Jae Guard tightens, doesn’t loosen.** Her dryness is armor, not just temperament (early/mid arc). So her withheld lushness and the suspicion-not-admiration rule are the **same wall doing the same job.** Her first real lush image about *him specifically* is a late-arc unlock, spent with the same care as the rueful “everything I’d want, it just sucks that it’s him” confession. Do not blow it early on scenery.
1. **The lush serves show-don’t-tell; it never becomes telling.** Do-yun’s atmosphere puts the reader *in the room* — it never steps outside the scene to flag significance or name his own arc. “The booth forgave nothing” = shown. “It felt like remembering, and that was the moment I knew” = cut. Lush texture, not lush editorializing.
1. **Audio-first still governs both channels.** Punctuation does the pacing for both; his triads must still read aloud cleanly in one Speechify voice (Dominic), hers in one (Geffen). Lush ≠ run-on; the held breath is built from structure the ear can hear, not from clauses the eye merely sees.

-----

## Drafting tell (quick gut-check per line)

- Do-yun POV, charged beat → is the texture *pouring* (his Se), or did it go flat and clipped (that’s a Jae line in the wrong chapter)?
- Jae POV → is the feeling getting *checked for the bill* before it lands, or did it bloom unearned (that’s a Do-yun line in the wrong chapter, or a late-arc unlock spent early)?
- Either POV → did the lush start *explaining* instead of *placing*? Cut to placement.
